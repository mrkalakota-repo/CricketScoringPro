The Final Verdict: "The Hybrid Pro Model"

The panel rejects a pure "Ads-only" or "Subscription-only" model. The winning path is a three-pronged hybrid strategy that respects the user while monetizing the heavy-use cases.

Strategic Roadmap:

  • Phase 1: League Premium (B2B)
    Target league organizers. Charge a flat $10–$50 per tournament for "Verified Status," custom league branding, and automated PDF tournament reports.
  • Phase 2: Individual "Player Pro" (B2C)
    A $4.99 one-time unlock for players. This gives them "Wagon Wheels," advanced form-tracking graphs, and Instagram-style shareable scorecard images.
  • Phase 3: Contextual Commerce (Affiliate)
    Partner with local sports shops. When a team is created, show a "Team Gear" section with affiliate links to bats and balls based on their GPS location.
  • Phase 4: Proximity Sponsorships
    Allow a local beverage or equipment brand to "Sponsor" the Live Feed for a specific 50-mile radius. (e.g., "This match is brought to you by Local Sports Store").

Panel Conclusion: Avoid annoying popup ads. Your app's strength is its integrity and offline reliability. Monetize the Leagues (who have budgets) and the Top 10% of Players (who have vanity/ambition), while keeping the basic scoring free for the masses.
